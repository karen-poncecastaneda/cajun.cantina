Appetizers

We sever a verity of Mexican and Cajun appetizers. Starting with our complimentary fresh homemade guacamole.

  • Guacamole: Mashed avocado mixed with lime juice, onions, tomatoes, cilantro, and seasoning, served with tortilla chips.
  • Salsa and Chips: Freshly made tomato-based salsa served with crispy tortilla chips.
  • Nachos: Tortilla chips topped with melted cheese, beans, jalape単os, sour cream, guacamole, and salsa.
  • Shrimp Cocktail (Coctel de Camarones): Poached shrimp served in a cocktail sauce with avocado, onions, cilantro, and lime juice.
  • Boudin Balls: Cajun sausage mixed with rice, onions, and spices, formed into balls, breaded, and fried until crispy, served with a spicy dipping sauce.
  • Cajun Crab Dip: Creamy dip made with lump crab meat, cream cheese, Cajun spices, and green onions, served warm with toasted French bread or crackers
  • Cajun Fried Pickles: Crispy dill pickle slices breaded in seasoned cornmeal, fried until golden brown, and served with a spicy dipping sauce.
  • Blackened Catfish Bites: Fresh catfish seasoned with Cajun spices, blackened in a hot skillet, and served with a creamy dipping sauce.

Entrees

  • Cajun Shrimp Tacos: Grilled shrimp seasoned with Cajun spices, served in soft tortillas with slaw and a spicy remoulade.
  • Blackened Fish Tostadas: Tostadas topped with blackened Cajun-seasoned fish, shredded lettuce, pico de gallo, and a drizzle of lime crema.
  • Cajun Chicken Enchiladas: Enchiladas filled with Cajun-spiced shredded chicken, rolled in corn tortillas, and baked with a spicy tomato sauce.
  • Cajun Carnitas Tacos:Tacos featuring slow-cooked Cajun-style pork carnitas, topped with pickled onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
  • Blackened Chicken Tamales: Tamales filled with blackened Cajun-seasoned chicken, wrapped in corn husks and steamed to perfection.
  • Ceviche: Raw seafood (often shrimp or fish) marinated in lime or lemon juice, mixed with tomatoes, onions, cilantro, and other seasonings.
  • Aguachile: Similar to ceviche, but the seafood is marinated in a spicy green chili sauce made with lime juice.
  • Pescado a la Veracruzana: Fish Veracruz-style, cooked with tomatoes, olives, capers, and peppers.
  • Gumbo: A hearty stew typically made with a strongly flavored stock, meat or shellfish, and vegetables. It often includes ingredients like okra, bell peppers, onions, and celery.
  • Crawfish Boil: Crawfish are boiled with a mix of spices, corn, potatoes, and sometimes other seafood. The boiled ingredients are then typically spread out on a table for a communal feast.
  • Jambalaya: A rice dish with a mix of meats (such as sausage, chicken, and sometimes seafood), vegetables, and seasonings.
  • Cajun Fried Chicken: Chicken seasoned with a blend of Cajun spices, then fried to a crispy perfection.

Drinks

  • Michelada: A beer-based cocktail made with beer, lime juice, assorted sauces (such as Worcestershire and hot sauce), spices, and peppers. It's often served in a salt-rimmed glass.
  • Horchata: A sweet rice-based drink flavored with cinnamon and sometimes vanilla.
  • Tejuino: A fermented beverage made from fermented corn dough, water, and piloncillo (unrefined sugar).
  • Jarritos: A popular brand of Mexican sodas with a variety of flavors such as tamarind, mandarin, and guava.
  • Jarritos: A popular brand of Mexican sodas with a variety of flavors such as tamarind, mandarin, and guava.
  • Paloma: A refreshing drink made with tequila, grapefruit soda (such as Jarritos or Squirt), and a splash of lime juice. It's typically served over ice and garnished with a wedge of lime.
  • Tequila Sunrise: A visually stunning cocktail made with tequila, orange juice, and grenadine. The grenadine creates a sunrise effect in the glass.
  • Pi単a Colada Mexicana: A Mexican twist on the classic Pi単a Colada, incorporating coconut cream, pineapple juice, and rum.
  • Tamarind Margarita: A twist on the classic margarita, this version includes tamarind juice for a sweet and tangy flavor.
  • Sangrita: Although not a cocktail on its own, Sangrita is often served alongside tequila. It's a spicy and flavorful mixture of tomato and citrus juices, sometimes with chili pepper.

Desserts

  • Tres Leches Cake: A sponge cake soaked in a mixture of three milks (evaporated milk, condensed milk, and heavy cream) and topped with whipped cream.
  • Churros: Fried dough pastries, often coated in cinnamon and sugar, served with chocolate sauce or caramel for dipping.
  • Flan: A creamy caramel custard dessert, often flavored with vanilla and topped with a layer of caramel.
  • Arroz con Leche: Mexican rice pudding made with rice, milk, sugar, and flavored with cinnamon and vanilla.
  • Bu単uelos: Fried dough fritters, typically sprinkled with sugar and cinnamon. They can be served with syrup or a piloncillo (unrefined sugar) sauce.
  • Capirotada: A traditional bread pudding made with layers of bread, cheese, nuts, and dried fruits, all soaked in a sweet syrup.
